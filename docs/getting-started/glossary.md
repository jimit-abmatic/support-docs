# Glossary of Terms

Master the language of account-based marketing. This glossary covers key terms you'll encounter in Abmatic AI and ABM strategy—organized alphabetically for quick reference.

:::tip Quick Navigation
Use your browser's search (Ctrl/Cmd + F) to find specific terms instantly.
:::

---

## A

### ABM (Account-Based Marketing)
A B2B marketing strategy that focuses resources on specific high-value target accounts rather than casting a wide net. Instead of generating leads and hoping some become customers, ABM identifies ideal companies first and markets directly to decision-makers.

**Why it matters:** Companies using ABM see 208% higher revenue from marketing efforts (ITSMA).

**In Abmatic:** The entire platform is built for ABM—from visitor identification to personalization to attribution.

---

### Account
A company or organization you're targeting or tracking. Each account can have multiple contacts (people) associated with it.

**Find it:** Reveal > Accounts, or Audiences > Accounts

---

### Account List / Target Group
A saved collection of accounts for campaign targeting. Create lists manually, import from CSV, sync from your CRM, or build dynamically with filters.

**Find it:** Audiences > Target Groups

---

### AI Agent
An AI-powered assistant that automates marketing and sales tasks:

| Agent | Purpose | Best For |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Alex (AI SDR)** | Automated prospecting and outreach | Sales teams needing research and email sequences |
| **Clara (AI ABM)** | Campaign execution and personalization | Marketers optimizing campaign performance |

**Find it:** AI Agents in the sidebar

---

### Agentic Chat
AI-powered website chat that answers visitor questions using your company and product knowledge. Engages prospects 24/7 without human intervention.

---

### Agentic Sequence
AI-generated email sequences for prospect outreach, personalized based on visitor attributes and behavior signals.

---

### Attribution
Connecting marketing activities to business outcomes. Abmatic tracks which campaigns influenced which accounts and opportunities—proving marketing ROI.

---

### Audience
A group of accounts or contacts for campaign targeting:

| Type | Behavior | Best For |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Static** | Fixed list that doesn't change | One-time campaigns, specific account lists |
| **Dynamic** | Auto-updates based on filter criteria | Ongoing campaigns, growing account pools |

---

## B

### Buying Committee
The group of people at a target account involved in purchase decisions. ABM strategies target multiple committee members to build consensus.

**Tip:** Use Contact Reveal to identify committee members visiting your site.

---

## C

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
Total cost to acquire a customer, including marketing and sales expenses.

**Why ABM helps:** Focused spend on high-value targets typically reduces CAC by 30-50%.

---

### Campaign
A coordinated marketing effort in Abmatic:

| Campaign Type | Channel | Use Case |
|---------------|---------|----------|
| **Website Personalization** | Your website | Tailor content to visitor segments |
| **Email Campaigns** | Email | Nurture sequences and announcements |
| **LinkedIn Ads** | LinkedIn | Target decision-makers by account |
| **Google Ads** | Google Search/Display | Retarget and expand reach |
| **CRM Campaigns** | Your CRM | Sync data and trigger workflows |

**Find it:** Campaigns in the sidebar

---

### Contact
An individual person at a target account. Contacts have attributes like name, email, job title, and company.

**Find it:** Reveal > Contacts

---

### Contact Reveal
Identify specific people visiting your website (not just their company) using email matching and data enrichment.

**Requires:** Contact Reveal credits
**Result:** Full contact profiles including email, title, and LinkedIn

---

### Control Group
In A/B testing, visitors who see your original, unmodified content. Used to measure the impact of personalization against a baseline.

**Default:** 20% of matching visitors see the control version

---

### Conversion
A desired action taken by a visitor—form submission, demo request, purchase, or custom event. Abmatic tracks conversions to measure campaign effectiveness and calculate ROI.

**Find it:** Conversions in the sidebar

---

### CRM (Customer Relationship Management)
Software for managing customer and prospect data. Abmatic integrates with:
- Salesforce
- HubSpot
- Pipedrive
- ActiveCampaign
- Outreach

---

## D

### Dashboard
A visual display of key metrics and KPIs. Create custom dashboards with drag-and-drop widgets to monitor what matters most.

**Find it:** Analytics > Dashboards

---

### Domain
A company's website address (e.g., acme.com). Abmatic uses domains as the primary way to match and identify companies.

---

### Dynamic Content
Website content that changes based on who's viewing it. Show different headlines, CTAs, or case studies to visitors from different industries, company sizes, or buying stages.

**Example:** "Trusted by 500+ technology companies" becomes "Trusted by 500+ healthcare organizations" for healthcare visitors.

---

### Dynamic Collection
An audience that automatically updates based on filter criteria. New visitors matching the criteria are added automatically—no manual list management required.

---

## E

### Engagement Score
A measure of how actively an account interacts with your website and content. Higher scores indicate stronger buying interest.

**Components:** Page views, time on site, content downloads, return visits, and more.

---

### Enrichment
Adding data to your records automatically:

| Record Type | Data Added |
|-------------|------------|
| **Accounts** | Industry, employee count, revenue, technology stack |
| **Contacts** | Job title, email, LinkedIn profile, seniority level |

---

## F

### Field Mapping
Configuration that connects Abmatic fields to your CRM fields. Ensures data syncs correctly between systems.

**Find it:** Integrations > [CRM] > Field Mapping

---

### Fill Mode
A sync setting where Abmatic only updates empty fields. Existing data is preserved.

**Best for:** Preserving manually entered data, respecting CRM as source of truth

---

### Filter
Conditions used to segment audiences. Combine multiple criteria with AND/OR logic.

**Example:** "Industry is Technology AND Employee Count > 500 AND Country is United States"

---

### Firmographic Data
Company attributes used for targeting and personalization:
- Industry
- Company size (employees)
- Annual revenue
- Location (HQ and offices)
- Technology stack

---

## I

### ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A description of the company type most likely to become a successful customer. Your ICP guides targeting and prioritization.

**Common ICP attributes:**
- Industry vertical
- Company size range
- Revenue range
- Geographic location
- Technology requirements

---

### Impression
One view of personalized content. Tracked to measure campaign reach and calculate conversion rates.

---

### Intent Data
Signals indicating a company is actively researching solutions like yours:

| Intent Type | Source | Signal Strength |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| **First-party** | Your website | Strongest—direct interest |
| **Third-party** | Research platforms | Moderate—broader interest |
| **Behavioral** | Content consumption | Context-dependent |

---

### IP Identification
Matching a website visitor's IP address to a company. This is how Abmatic identifies which businesses visit your site.

**Identification rate:** 40-80% depending on traffic type (office IPs identify better than remote workers)

---

## L

### Lead
A person who has shown interest but isn't yet qualified. In Salesforce, leads are separate from contacts until converted.

---

### Lift
The improvement in performance from personalization compared to the control group, expressed as a percentage.

**Example:** "23% lift in conversions" means 23% more conversions than visitors who saw the original content.

---

### LinkedIn DMP
LinkedIn's Data Management Platform for advertising. Push account lists from Abmatic to LinkedIn DMP for matched audience targeting.

**Typical match rate:** 60-80%

---

## M

### Match Rate
The percentage of your list that successfully matches on an ad platform.

**Example:** Push 1,000 accounts to LinkedIn, 700 match = 70% match rate

**Improve match rates:** Include company domain and LinkedIn Company URL when available.

---

### MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)
A lead that meets marketing's criteria and is ready to pass to sales. Criteria vary by company but typically include engagement level and fit score.

---

## O

### Overwrite Mode
A sync setting where Abmatic always updates fields with its data, even if the CRM field has a value.

**Use with care:** Can overwrite manually entered data. Best for fields where Abmatic is the source of truth.

---

## P

### Page Editor
Abmatic's visual tool for creating personalized experiences. Edit any webpage without code—change text, images, CTAs, and entire sections.

**Find it:** Open a campaign page > Click "Edit"

---

### Personalization
Customizing content based on visitor attributes:

| Personalization Type | Example |
|---------------------|---------|
| **Company name** | "Welcome, Acme Corp" |
| **Industry** | Industry-specific case studies |
| **Company size** | Enterprise vs. SMB messaging |
| **Behavior** | Content based on pages viewed |
| **CRM data** | Stage-specific messaging |

---

### Personalization Variable
A placeholder replaced with visitor-specific data at runtime.

| Variable | Output |
|----------|--------|
| `{{mt_company}}` | Visitor's company name |
| `{{mt_industry}}` | Visitor's industry |
| `{{mt_city}}` | Visitor's city |

---

### Pipeline
The total value of deals in your sales process. Abmatic tracks how campaigns influence pipeline creation and movement.

---

### Publish
Making personalization changes live on your website. Until published, changes remain in draft mode—visible only in preview.

---

## R

### Rate Limit
A restriction on API requests per time period. Abmatic enforces rate limits to ensure system stability.

**Default limits:** 100 requests/minute for most endpoints

---

### Report
A saved query of your Abmatic data for analysis. Include metrics, dimensions, filters, and visualizations.

**Find it:** Analytics > Reports

---

### Reveal
Abmatic's visitor identification feature:

| Feature | What It Shows | Requirements |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| **Company Reveal** | Which businesses visit your site | Tracking script installed |
| **Contact Reveal** | Specific people (name, email, title) | Contact Reveal credits |

**Find it:** Reveal in the sidebar

---

## S

### Segment
A subset of your audience defined by specific criteria. Used for targeting in campaigns and analysis in reports.

---

### SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)
A lead that sales has accepted and is actively working. Higher intent or better fit than an MQL.

---

### Statistical Significance
The confidence level that an A/B test result is real, not random chance. Abmatic shows significance in campaign analytics—aim for 95%+ before making decisions.

---

### Sync
Data transfer between Abmatic and another system:

| Sync Type | Direction | Use Case |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Push** | Abmatic → CRM | Send identified visitors to sales |
| **Pull** | CRM → Abmatic | Import target accounts |
| **Bidirectional** | Both | Keep systems aligned |

---

## T

### Target Account
A high-priority company you've identified as a prospect. Target accounts match your ICP and are the focus of ABM campaigns.

---

### Target Group
A collection of accounts and/or contacts bundled together for campaign targeting. Can be static or dynamic.

---

### Tracking Script
A JavaScript snippet installed on your website that enables visitor identification and personalization.

```html
<script async src="https://clients.abmatic.ai/YOUR_ID.js"></script>
```

**Installation time:** Under 5 minutes
**Impact:** Minimal—async loading doesn't slow your site

---

## U

### UTM Parameters
URL tags for tracking marketing campaign performance:

| Parameter | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| `utm_source` | Traffic source | linkedin, google, email |
| `utm_medium` | Marketing medium | cpc, social, email |
| `utm_campaign` | Campaign name | q4-abm-campaign |
| `utm_content` | Content variant | cta-a, banner-1 |
| `utm_term` | Search keywords | abm software |

---

## V

### Visitor
Someone who comes to your website:

| Visitor Type | Identification Level | Data Available |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Anonymous** | Company only | Firmographics, behavior |
| **Known** | Individual person | Full profile, contact info |

---

### Visitor Identification
The process of determining which company (and potentially which person) visits your website. The foundation of ABM.

---

## W

### Webhook
An automated message sent from Abmatic to your systems when events occur:
- New visitor identified
- Conversion tracked
- Account reaches engagement threshold

**Use case:** Trigger real-time alerts and workflows in your stack.

---

### Widget
A component on a dashboard displaying a specific metric, chart, or data table. Drag and drop to customize your view.
