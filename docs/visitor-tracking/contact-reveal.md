# Contact Reveal

Stop wondering "who" is visiting your website. Contact Reveal identifies the actual people behind anonymous website visits, giving you verified contact information for real decision-makers at companies researching your solution.

## The Business Impact

Company identification tells you "someone from Quantum Metric visited your pricing page." Contact Reveal goes further — it tells you that Stacy Carrier, Senior Manager of Web Experience Engineering at Quantum Metric, visited your pricing page with a website engagement score of 52. That is the difference between knowing a company is interested and knowing exactly who to call.

| Before Contact Reveal | With Contact Reveal |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| "A company visited our pricing page" | "Stacy Carrier, Senior Manager at Quantum Metric, visited pricing" |
| Cold outreach to generic contacts | Warm outreach to engaged individuals |
| Guessing who to contact | Knowing exactly who to reach |
| Days to build prospect lists | Instant access to verified contacts |
| Low response rates | Higher engagement from informed outreach |

## How Contact Reveal Works

Contact Reveal uses multiple data sources to identify individual visitors. Here is what happens behind the scenes:

### The Identification Process

```
Website Visit --> IP Identification --> Contact Database Match --> Verified Contact
```

When a visitor arrives on your site, Abmatic first identifies their company (via IP matching). Then it cross-references the company data with professional contact databases, form submission history, and email click-through data to determine the specific individual.

### Identification Methods and What They Mean for Your Outreach

| Method | How It Works | Confidence | Example |
|--------|-------------|------------|---------|
| **Form Submissions** | Visitor fills out any form on your site | Very High | A demo request connects their email and name to all browsing history |
| **Email Link Clicks** | Visitor clicks a link in your marketing email | Very High | The click-through URL identifies them, connecting to their session |
| **IP + Database Match** | IP identification combined with professional contact databases | High to Moderate | Company IP matched to known employees in that role |
| **Behavioral Signals** | Session patterns matched to known visitor profiles | Moderate | Repeat visit patterns consistent with a known professional |

:::tip Why This Matters for Sales
A contact identified through a form submission or email click is someone who has already engaged with your brand. A contact identified through IP + database matching is someone whose company is showing interest. Both are valuable, but tailor your outreach accordingly — the first group expects to hear from you, the second needs a softer approach.
:::

### Temperature Indicators

Temperature gives you an instant read on how engaged a contact is:

| Temperature | Meaning | Recommended Action |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Hot** | Highly active, frequent recent visits, deep engagement | Drop everything and reach out now |
| **Warm** | Active engagement, recent visits | Prioritize for outreach this week |
| **Cold** | Past engagement, not recently active | Re-engagement campaign or nurture sequence |
| **None** | New or limited data | Monitor and add to awareness campaigns |

## Taking Action on Revealed Contacts

### Add to Contact Lists

Build targeted lists for campaigns:

1. Select contacts using checkboxes
2. Click **Add to Contacts**
3. Choose an existing list or create new

**Pro tip:** Create lists by engagement level—separate "Hot Leads" from "Nurture" contacts.

### Push to CRM

Sync valuable contacts to your CRM automatically:

1. Select contacts to sync
2. Click **Push to CRM**
3. Map fields to your CRM schema
4. Complete the sync

**Synced data includes:**
- Contact information (name, email, title)
- Company association
- Engagement score
- Page views and activity

### Export Data

Download contacts for use in other tools:

1. Select contacts (or export all)
2. Click **Export**
3. Download CSV file

## Configuring Contact Reveal

Optimize Contact Reveal for your needs in **Settings** > **Contact Reveal**.

![Contact Reveal settings showing accuracy slider and filter configuration](/img/screenshots/reveal-settings.png)

*The Contact Reveal settings page. The Accuracy slider controls the trade-off between volume and precision — slide left for "High Volume / Low Accuracy" or right for "Low Volume / High Accuracy." Below, the Filters section lets you set rules (Selector, Operator, Value) to focus reveals on contacts that match your ideal customer profile. Click "+ RULE" to add additional filter criteria.*

### Accuracy vs. Volume

The accuracy slider is the most important setting for Contact Reveal. It controls the confidence threshold for identifying contacts:

| Setting | Accuracy | Volume | Best For |
|---------|----------|--------|----------|
| **10-30%** | Lower | Higher | Maximum coverage, broad outreach, building awareness lists |
| **40-60%** | Balanced | Balanced | General B2B use (recommended for most companies) |
| **70-100%** | Higher | Lower | High-value accounts, precision targeting, direct sales outreach |

The default is **50%** which provides a good balance of volume and accuracy for most B2B companies.

:::tip Finding Your Sweet Spot
Start at 50% and monitor the quality of revealed contacts for a week. If you are seeing too many irrelevant contacts, increase the threshold. If you need more volume for advertising audiences, lower it. You can adjust at any time without losing existing data.
:::

### Reveal Filters

Filters let you focus reveals on contacts that match your ideal customer profile. This saves credits and ensures your team only sees relevant prospects.

**Example filters:**
- Company size > 100 employees (skip very small businesses)
- Industry = Technology or Finance (your target verticals)
- Country = United States (your primary market)

Click **+ RULE** to add criteria, then **SAVE** to apply. Filters take effect immediately for new reveals.

## Best Practices for Acting on Revealed Contacts

### Do's

| Practice | Why | Example |
|----------|-----|---------|
| **Prioritize by temperature** | Hot and Warm contacts are actively engaged | Reach out to Warm contacts within 24 hours |
| **Cross-reference with accounts** | Connect contacts to company context | "Stacy at Quantum Metric viewed pricing — and 8 other people at that company are in our system" |
| **Personalize outreach** | Reference the pages they viewed | "I noticed your team has been researching our enterprise features..." |
| **Sync to CRM quickly** | Strike while interest is high | Push Hot/Warm contacts to your CRM the same day they are revealed |
| **Set up reveal filters** | Focus on ICP contacts only | Only reveal contacts at companies with 100+ employees in your target industries |

### Don'ts

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Contacting every revealed contact** | Prioritization beats volume — focus on quality |
| **Generic outreach** | You have behavior data — use it to be relevant |
| **Ignoring engagement scores** | A score of 52 means something very different from a score of 3 |
| **Letting contacts go stale** | Act within days, not weeks — intent decays fast |
| **Reaching out to Very Low confidence contacts with hard sells** | Use softer approaches for lower-confidence matches |

## High-Impact Workflows

### 1. "Identify the actual people behind anonymous website visits"

This is the core Contact Reveal workflow — turning anonymous traffic into named, reachable prospects:

```
Anonymous website visitor
    --> Abmatic identifies company via IP
    --> Contact database match reveals individual
    --> Contact appears in Reveal with email, title, and engagement score
    --> Sales rep reaches out with personalized context
```

### 2. Sales Pipeline Acceleration

When a revealed contact shows high intent, move fast:

```
Revealed Contact (Pricing Page + Warm/Hot Temperature)
    --> Push to CRM as Lead --> Assign to Sales Rep
    --> Rep checks engagement history (which pages, how many visits)
    --> Personalized Outreach: "I noticed you were researching [specific feature]..."
```

### 3. "Build a target list from your most engaged visitors"

Use Contact Reveal to build your best-possible outreach list:

1. Open Reveal > Contacts
2. Sort by Website Engagement Score (highest first)
3. Filter by Temperature = Warm or Hot
4. Select the top contacts and add to a Contact List
5. Export to your CRM or use as an audience for LinkedIn campaigns

### 4. ABM Account Penetration (Map the Buying Committee)

When you identify a target account, use Contact Reveal to find every person at that company:

```
Target Account visits --> Click into Account --> Contacts tab
    --> See all identified contacts by department (Marketing, Engineering, Sales)
    --> Add to Account-Based Campaign
    --> Multi-threaded outreach to the full buying committee
```

### 5. Event Follow-Up

```
Webinar registration page visitors
    --> Reveal attendee contacts --> Add to event list
    --> Pre-event: "Looking forward to seeing you..."
    --> Post-event: "Thanks for joining..."
```

## Troubleshooting

### Low Reveal Volume

| Issue | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Few contacts revealed | Accuracy too high | Lower accuracy setting to 40-50% |
| Wrong contacts | No reveal filters | Add ICP filters |
| Missing contacts | Traffic from VPNs | Normal—some traffic unidentifiable |

### Data Quality Issues

| Issue | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Outdated job titles | Database lag | Verify on LinkedIn before outreach |
| Wrong company | Multi-company IPs | Cross-reference with page behavior |
| Missing engagement | New visitor | Wait for more activity |

### CRM Sync Problems

| Issue | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Push fails | Field mapping issue | Check required fields in CRM |
| Duplicates created | No matching rule | Enable duplicate detection |
| Data not syncing | Integration disconnected | Reconnect in Settings |
